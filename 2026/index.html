Workshop Description

The 1st ACNS Workshop on Intelligent and Secure Computing (ACNS-ISC 2026) provides a specialized venue for disseminating advanced research at the intersection of intelligent computing and secure computing. As AI-driven systems increasingly operate in security-critical environments, the workshop seeks contributions that address fundamental challenges in designing models and architectures that are both effective and trustworthy.

Topics of interest include machine learning–based threat detection, adversarial machine learning, privacy-preserving model training, secure federated learning, cryptographic mechanisms for intelligent systems, and security of emerging infrastructures such as IoT, cyber-physical systems, and cloud–edge platforms.

ACNS-ISC 2026 welcomes theoretical advances, practical systems, and empirical studies that strengthen the foundations of secure intelligent computing and promote interdisciplinary collaboration across AI, cryptography, and cybersecurity.
